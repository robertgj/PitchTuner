Detailed Description

Implemetation of Butterworth filters as a cascade of second-order state-space sections.

This file implements Butterworth second- and third-order high-pass filters and a Butterworth fourth-order low-pass filter as a cascade of low-noise second-order state-variable sections or as the parallel combination of two all-pass state-variable sections.

For a low-pass Butterworth filter, the squared magnitude response is [1] :

\[ \left|\hat{H}(s)\right|^{2} = \frac{1}{1+\omega^{2n}} \]

The \(2n\) poles of the Butterworth squared-magnitude response are evenly spaced around a circle in the \(s\)-plane. In the left half \(s\)-plane the angles are:

\[ \theta_{k}=\frac{\pi}{2}\left(1+\frac{2k-1}{n}\right)\;,\quad 1\le k\le n \]

If the cutoff frequency in the \(s\)-plane is \(\omega_{c}\) then first order low-pass sections are:

\[ \frac{\omega_{c}}{s+\omega_{c}} \]

and second order low-pass sections are:

\[ \frac{\omega_{c}^{2}}{s^{2}-2\omega_{c}\cos\theta_{k}s+\omega_{c}^{2}} \]

The \(s\)-plane Butterworth response is transformed to the \(z\)-plane by:

\[ H(z) = \hat{H}\left(\frac{1-z^{-1}}{1+z^{-1}}\right) \]

On the \(z\)-plane unit circle, \(z=e^{\imath\Omega T}\), where \(T\) is the sampling interval and:

\[ H(e^{\imath\Omega T}) = \hat{H}\left(\imath\tan\frac{\Omega T}{2}\right) \]

For each filter, first find the Butterworth pole locations and the corresponding transfer function for each second-order section:

\[ H(z)=d+\frac{q_1 z^{-1} + q_2 z^{-2}}{1 + p_1 z^{-1} + p_2 z^{-2}} \]

Next, find the optimal low-noise second order state variable sections [2] or, for high-pass filters the Stoyanov [3] LS1 and LS2a, or Gray-and-Markel [4] GM1 and GM2, first and second order state variable sections. The Stoyanov and Gray-and-Markel all-pass filters are "structurally lossless" in the sense of Vaidyanathan et al. [5] ; the all-pass response is retained when the coefficients are truncated.

See:

[1] Appendix 6A, "Design of Analog Filter Prototypes", in "Digital Signal Processing", R. A. Roberts and C. T. Mullis, Addison-Wesley, 1987, ISBN 0-201-16350-0

[2] "New second-order state-space structures for realizing low round off noise digital filters", B. W. Bomar, IEEE Transactions on Acoustics, Speech and Signal Processing, Vol. ASSP-33, pp. 106-110, Feb. 1985

[3] "Design and Realization of Efficient IIR Digital Filter Structures Based on Sensitivity Minimizations", G. Stoyanov, Z. Nikolova, K. Ivanova and V. Anzova, Proceedings TELSIKS 2007, pp. 299-308, Sept. 2007

[4] "Digital Lattice And Ladder Filter Synthesis", A. H. Gray and J. D. Markel, IEEE Transactions on Audio and Electroacoustics, Vol. 21, No. 6, pp. 491-500, Dec. 1973

[5] "A New Approach to the Realization of Low-Sensitivity IIR Digital Filters", P. P. Vaidyanathan,S. K. Mitra and Y. Nuevo, IEEE Transactions on Acoustics, Speech and Signal Processing, Vol. ASSP-34, No. 2, pp. 350-361, April 1986

Testing with Octave

  1. Create a noise sequence in Octave:
    Fs = 48000
    N = Fs*10
    n = rand(N,1)-0.5;
    n = 0.5*n/std(n);
    audiowrite('noise.wav',n,Fs);
  2. Run Butterworth_test:
    bin/ButterworthFilter_test --inFile noise.wav --outFile y.wav
    --seconds 10 --cutoff 100 --high_pass_GM
  3. Analyse the result in Octave:
    [y,Fs]=audioread('y.wav');
    Nfreq=16384
    td=xspec(n,y,2*Nfreq);
    plot((0:(Nfreq-1))*Fs/(2*Nfreq), 20*log10(abs(td(1:Nfreq))))
    axis([0 200 -50 5]);
